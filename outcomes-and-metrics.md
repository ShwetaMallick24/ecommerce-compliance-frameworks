## How These Metrics Connect to Framework Design

These results didn't happen in isolation — they were direct consequences of specific design decisions:

**30% faster listing-to-live** came from the risk tiering model separating low-risk products (fast track) from high-risk products (rigorous track). Without tiering, every product moves at the speed of the slowest review process.

**One-third fewer takedowns** came from the seller onboarding playbook front-loading clarity. When sellers understand requirements before they list — not after — they launch compliant products the first time.

**5–7% conversion lift** came from making invisible compliance visible through the trust badge. Buyer behavior changes when trust signals are concrete and specific, not generic.

**3x faster category expansion** came from building parallel review tracks for Tier 3 products, so legal review and lab certification ran simultaneously rather than sequentially.

## What the Numbers Don't Capture

Metrics tell part of the story. A few things that don't show up in dashboards but matter for TPM work:

- **Seller trust:** Sellers who understood the system advocated for the platform. Sellers who felt blindsided by takedowns churned. The playbook reduced churn in regulated categories by improving clarity, even before the quantitative metrics moved.
- **Internal alignment:** Getting 50+ stakeholders across engineering, legal, and operations to agree on a single risk framework required sustained negotiation. That alignment was a prerequisite for any of the speed improvements.
- **Competitive signal:** Competitors adopting the trust badge model within months is the strongest validation that the design was right. Imitation is the clearest market signal.
